Thomas Edison: The Prolific Inventor

Thomas Edison, born in 1847 in Milan, Ohio, was an extraordinary inventor whose work has had a profound impact on the modern world. With over 1,000 patents to his name, Edison's innovations include the phonograph, the motion picture camera, and perhaps most famously, the practical electric light bulb.

Thomas Edison in his laboratory

One of Edison's key contributions was the establishment of the first industrial research lab, which became a model for future innovation and development. His ability to turn ideas into practical applications not only transformed industries but also laid the groundwork for the technological advances of the 20th century and beyond.
