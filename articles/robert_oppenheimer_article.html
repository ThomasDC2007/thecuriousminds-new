J. Robert Oppenheimer: The Father of the Atomic Bomb

J. Robert Oppenheimer, born in 1904 in New York City, was an American theoretical physicist best known for his role as the scientific director of the Manhattan Project, the World War II undertaking that developed the first nuclear weapons. Oppenheimer's leadership and expertise were crucial in the successful creation and testing of the atomic bomb.

J. Robert Oppenheimer at Los Alamos

Oppenheimer's work on the Manhattan Project not only changed the course of the war but also ushered in a new era of nuclear physics and international relations. His famous quote, "Now I am become Death, the destroyer of worlds," reflects the profound and complex legacy of his contributions to science and their impact on humanity.
