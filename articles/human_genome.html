The Human Genome Project

The Human Genome Project (HGP) was an international research endeavor that aimed to map all the genes of the human genome. Initiated in 1990, it involved thousands of scientists from across the globe. The project concluded in 2003, revealing that humans have approximately 20,000-25,000 genes. This monumental achievement has paved the way for advancements in medicine, biotechnology, and our understanding of human evolution.

Scientists working on genome sequencing

The HGP was a collaborative effort between numerous institutions and countries, including the United States, the United Kingdom, Japan, France, Germany, and China. The project was publicly funded and also saw the involvement of private companies like Celera Genomics. The combined efforts of these entities have led to significant breakthroughs in personalized medicine and genetic research.
